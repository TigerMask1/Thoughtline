Traveling for a week with nothing but a carry-on bag and a personal item is entirely doable, and it spares you the headache of lost luggage and long baggage claim lines. The secret isn't buying miniature versions of everything you own; it is selecting versatile items and packing them strategically. This guide breaks down exactly what to pack for a seven-day trip, assuming temperate weather, using a system that fits standard airline limits.

## The Luggage and the Personal Item Setup

Before you pack a single shirt, you need to understand your physical space constraints. Most major domestic and international airlines limit carry-on bags to 22 x 14 x 9 inches (56 x 36 x 23 cm). Your personal item—like a small backpack or tote—must fit under the seat in front of you, which generally means keeping it under 18 x 14 x 8 inches.

For your main bag, a lightweight, soft-sided travel pack like the [Patagonia](https://www.patagonia.com) Black Hole MLC or a standard two-wheel spinner suitcase works best. To maximize this space, use compression packing cubes. Instead of just organizing your gear, double-zipper compression cubes squeeze out excess air, saving up to 30% of your bag's total volume. Group your shirts in one medium cube, bottoms in a second cube, and undergarments in a smaller third cube.

## The 5-4-3-2-1 Clothing Formula

To avoid overpacking clothes "just in case," use a modified version of the classic travel countdown rule. Choose a single cohesive color palette (such as navy, grey, and white, or olive, black, and tan) so that every top matches every bottom. 

* **5 Tops:** Pack three basic t-shirts or tees (merino wool is highly recommended because it naturally resists odors and dries quickly), one button-down shirt or blouse, and one lightweight sweater or fleece for layering.
* **4 Bottoms:** Bring one pair of comfortable travel pants or jeans, one pair of chinos or a skirt, one pair of athletic shorts, and one pair of casual shorts. Wear your heaviest, bulkiest pants on the plane.
* **3 Pairs of Underwear and Socks (With a Catch):** Pack four pairs of socks and four pairs of underwear, planning to do a quick sink-wash mid-week. Alternatively, pack seven of each if they are lightweight synthetic fabrics that roll down to the size of a lemon.
* **2 Pairs of Shoes:** Wear your bulkiest shoes (like supportive walking sneakers) on the plane. Pack one lighter, flatter pair (like loafers, sandals, or clean white low-top sneakers) at the very bottom of your bag near the wheels.
* **1 Outer Layer:** Bring a versatile, packable rain jacket or utility coat. Wear or carry this onto the plane to save valuable suitcase space.

## Smart Toiletries and the 3-1-1 Rule

To pass airport security without delay, your liquids must comply with the [TSA 3-1-1 liquids rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3-1-1_liquids_rule). This means all liquids, gels, and aerosols must be in containers of 3.4 ounces (100ml) or less, and all fit inside a single, clear, quart-sized bag.

To save precious liquid space, swap out traditional products for solid alternatives:

* **Solid shampoo and conditioner bars:** These are highly concentrated, last for dozens of washes, and do not count toward your liquid limit.
* **Toothpaste tablets:** These dry tablets activate when you chew them with water, eliminating the need for a messy travel tube.
* **Solid stick sunscreen and deodorant:** Stick formulations are exempt from the liquids bag, freeing up space for face serums, contact lens solution, and toothpaste.

For the liquids you cannot swap, use soft, squeezable silicone travel bottles. Fill them only 80% full to allow room for air expansion during cabin pressure changes, preventing messy leaks.

## Tech and Personal Item Essentials

Your personal item should hold anything you need to access during transit. Keep your electronics minimal and consolidated. Instead of bringing a laptop, a tablet, and an e-reader, choose one primary device. 

Pack a universal travel adapter with built-in USB ports. This allows you to charge your phone, watch, and headphones simultaneously from a single wall outlet, saving you from carrying multiple bulky charging bricks. Finally, keep a lightweight, 10,000mAh external battery pack in an easily accessible pocket so your phone stays charged while navigating unfamiliar cities.
