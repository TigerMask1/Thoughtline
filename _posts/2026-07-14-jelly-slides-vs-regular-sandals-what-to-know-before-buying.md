Deciding on summer footwear used to be simple, but the revival of 1980s retro fashion has brought a classic competitor back to the shoe racks: [jelly shoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jelly_shoes) and slides. If you are torn between a pair of bright, translucent jelly slides and traditional sandals made of leather, cork, or EVA foam, it is easy to make the wrong choice based on looks alone. 

While both options keep your feet cool, they perform differently when it comes to support, sweat management, and long-term durability. Here is what you need to know about how these materials behave on your feet before you spend your money.

## Material Differences: PVC vs. Leather and EVA

The primary difference between these two categories is the material. Jelly slides are molded from polyvinyl chloride (PVC), a synthetic plastic. PVC is completely non-porous, highly flexible, and waterproof. It does not absorb moisture, which is both a benefit and a drawback.

Regular sandals, on the other hand, utilize a wider variety of materials. High-quality traditional sandals often feature leather straps, cork footbeds, or molded ethylene-vinyl acetate (EVA) foam. EVA is a lightweight plastic copolymer that offers significantly more cushioning than standard PVC. Unlike PVC, natural materials like leather and cork will break in over time, molding specifically to the shape of your footprint.

## Comfort, Cushioning, and Foot Support

If you plan to walk more than a mile or two at a time, support should be your primary concern. 

*   **Jelly Slides:** Because they are molded from a single piece of plastic, most jelly slides offer very little arch support. They have a flat, slightly bouncy footbed that is fine for quick trips but lacks the structural integrity required for all-day walking. 
*   **Regular Sandals:** Brands like [Birkenstock](https://www.birkenstock.com) design sandals with contoured cork and latex footbeds that actively support your arches and distribute your body weight evenly. Even basic sport sandals usually feature multi-layered midsoles designed to absorb shock. 

If you suffer from plantar fasciitis or general foot fatigue, a traditional supportive sandal will always outperform a flat jelly slide.

## The Sweat and Friction Factor

How your feet react to heat is a major differentiator between these shoe types. Because PVC plastic is entirely non-breathable, wearing jelly slides on a hot day will inevitably cause your feet to sweat. 

When sweat pools on a smooth plastic surface, it creates two problems: your feet will slide around inside the shoe, and the wet plastic will rub against your skin. This friction is a recipe for painful blisters, particularly on the bridge of your foot or between your toes. 

Regular sandals made of leather or fabric webbing absorb minor amounts of moisture, keeping your feet drier. Even EVA foam sandals, while plastic-based, are often textured to prevent your foot from sliding around when wet.

## Durability and Maintenance

When it comes to cleaning, jelly slides win easily. If you get mud, sand, or saltwater on your jellies, you can simply hose them off in the backyard or wash them in the sink with dish soap. They dry completely in minutes.

Traditional sandals require much more maintenance:
*   **Leather:** Can crack if it gets soaked and dries out without conditioner.
*   **Cork:** Requires occasional sealant to prevent water damage and crumbling.
*   **Suede:** Easily stained by water and dirt.

However, in terms of lifespan, high-quality regular sandals take the lead. While a cheap pair of jelly slides might split at the strap after a single season of heavy use, well-made leather or cork sandals can last for several years and can sometimes even be resoled.

## Which Should You Buy?

To make your final decision, look at how you plan to use the shoes:

**Choose jelly slides if:** You need a budget-friendly shoe for the public pool, the beach, or short walks. They are excellent water shoes, easy to pack, and work well as quick slip-ons for running errands or gardening.

**Choose regular sandals if:** You need a reliable shoe for summer travel, sightseeing, or any activity where you will be walking more than 3,000 steps a day. The investment in better support and breathability will save your feet from aches and blisters.
