Hosting a build-your-own taco bar is one of the easiest ways to feed a crowd without spending a fortune. However, going to the grocery store without a clear split between wholesale buys and local produce leads to inflated grocery bills or wasted food. The secret to keeping costs around $4 to $6 per person is knowing which heavy-hitting staples to buy in bulk and which perishable toppings to buy fresh in small quantities.

Here is a budget-conscious shopping plan and portion guide designed for a 12-to-15-person taco bar potluck.

## Portion Math Per Guest

Before heading out, calculate your overall food quantities based on realistic eating habits. Guests usually build two or three tacos, plus a side of chips, rice, and beans.

- **Tortillas:** 3 small street-taco size (5-inch) or 2 medium (7-to-8-inch) per person.
- **Protein:** 4 to 5 ounces of raw meat (yields about 3 to 4 ounces cooked) per person.
- **Rice and Beans:** 1/3 cup cooked rice and 1/3 cup beans per person.
- **Shredded Cheese:** 1.5 to 2 ounces per person.
- **Salsa and Guacamole:** 2 ounces of salsa and 2 ounces of guacamole per person.
- **Chips:** 1.5 to 2 ounces (about two big handfuls) per person.

## What to Buy in Bulk (Wholesale & Warehouse Clubs)

Buying high-volume, shelf-stable, or heavy-weight items at clubs like [Costco](https://www.costco.com) or [Sam's Club](https://www.samsclub.com) cuts your cost per serving in half compared to standard supermarket prices.

- **Tortillas:** Look for multi-packs of 50 to 100 corn tortillas or 30-to-40-count flour tortillas. Extra corn tortillas freeze easily if unopened.
- **Pork Shoulder or Boneless Chicken Thighs:** Skip expensive beef cuts or boneless chicken breasts. A pork shoulder for slow-cooked [carnitas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnitas) costs roughly $2.00 to $2.50 per pound, and boneless chicken thighs stay juicy in a slow cooker for hours without drying out.
- **Rice and Dried or Canned Beans:** A 5 lb bag of long-grain white rice costs pennies per cup cooked. Pair it with a 6-lb #10 bulk can of black or pinto beans (or dry beans if cooking from scratch).
- **Shredded Cheese:** Pick up a 2 lb to 5 lb bag of Mexican blend or Monterey Jack cheese. Pre-shredded bulk cheese saves significant time and costs far less per ounce than small 8 oz bags.
- **Tortilla Chips and Jarred Salsa:** Get a 2 lb bag of restaurant-style chips and a twin-pack of jarred salsa (one mild, one medium). You can easily doctor jarred salsa with fresh cilantro and lime juice later.

## What to Buy Fresh (Local Supermarket or Produce Stand)

Bulk warehouses fail on delicate produce. You end up paying for a six-pack of cilantro or a dozen avocados that go bad before you can use them. Buy these fresh items in precise amounts at a local grocery store or neighborhood Mexican market:

- **Fresh Cilantro:** 2 to 3 bunches (often $0.50 to $0.80 each). Essential for stirring into warm rice, fresh salsa, and top-tier presentation.
- **Limes:** 8 to 10 fresh limes. Freshly squeezed lime juice instantly brightens up canned beans, heavy meat dishes, and store-bought guacamole.
- **White or Red Onions:** 2 large onions. Chop half a white onion finely for raw topping mix, and slice the rest to cook down into your meats or beans.
- **Avocados:** 4 to 5 fresh avocados. Buy them firm a few days in advance so they ripen naturally, or pick up a smaller single tub of fresh prepared guacamole on the day of the party. Pre-made bulk tubs of guacamole tend to turn brown quickly once opened.
- **Sour Cream or Mexican Crema:** One 16 oz tub. Most guests only take a small dollop, so a single standard container is plenty for 15 people.
- **Fresh Jalapeños:** 3 to 4 peppers. Dice one for those who like heat, and slice the rest for garnishing trays.

## Master Shopping List for 12–15 Guests

Take this exact checklist with you to keep your budget under control:

**Bulk Items:**
- 45–50 street-size corn tortillas or 30 flour tortillas
- 4 to 5 lbs pork shoulder or boneless chicken thighs
- 2 lbs long-grain white rice
- 1 large (#10 size) can pinto or black beans (approx. 6 lbs)
- 2 lbs shredded Mexican blend cheese
- 1 large bag (2 lbs) tortilla chips
- 1 large jar (24–32 oz) salsa

**Fresh Items:**
- 2 bunches cilantro
- 8–10 limes
- 2 large white onions
- 4–5 avocados
- 1 tub (16 oz) sour cream or crema
- 3 fresh jalapeños
