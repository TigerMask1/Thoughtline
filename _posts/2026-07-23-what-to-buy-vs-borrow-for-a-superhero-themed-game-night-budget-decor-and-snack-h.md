Hosting a comic book or superhero game night doesn't require blowing fifty dollars on licensed cardboard cutouts that end up in the trash at midnight. With a little strategic borrowing and a handful of dollar-store supplies, you can transform your living room into the Hall of Justice without exceeding a $35 total budget.

Here is practical guidance on where to split your budget, along with high-impact decor and snack ideas you can pull off in under an hour.

## What to Borrow (Save Your Money Here)

Before buying anything, ask your player group to bring specific items. Gamer friends usually love contributing if you give them clear assignments.

- **The Games:** Don't buy a $60 specialty game just for one evening. Ask your group to bring titles like [Marvel Champions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marvel_Champions:_The_Card_Game), *Codenames: Marvel*, or superhero tabletop RPG rulebooks. If you are doing a video game tournament (like *Injustice 2* or *Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3*), borrow extra controllers and console docks.
- **Primary Color Serving Bowls:** Superhero themes rely heavily on bold primary colors—red, yellow, and royal blue. Ask guests if they have plastic or ceramic serving bowls in those solid colors so you don't have to buy disposable plastic ones.
- **A Portable Projector or Extra Monitor:** If someone in your group has a small LED projector, borrow it to display vintage comic panels, classic cartoon loops, or muted superhero films against a blank wall for low-effort ambient visual atmosphere.

## What to Buy (Where a Few Dollars Go a Long Way)

Spend your small budget strictly on versatile base materials that create visual impact through scale rather than fine detail.

- **Plastic Tablecloth Rolls ($3–$4 each):** Buy one black, one bright yellow, and one royal blue tablecloth from a local party supply store.
- **Poster Board ($1 per sheet):** Grab 3 to 4 sheets of white and yellow poster board and a thick black permanent marker.
- **Green Candy Melts ($3–$4):** Essential for quick, themed snack customization in the baking aisle.

## Budget Decor Hacks: High Impact, Low Effort

Instead of buying pre-printed character banners, lean into the iconic visual language of classic [comic books](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comic_book): speech bubbles, action words, and bold silhouettes.

### The $8 Cardstock City Skyline
Hang a royal blue or dark night-sky tablecloth across your main wall or behind the snack table. Cut black poster board into simple rectangular silhouettes of varying heights to look like city buildings. Tape them along the bottom third of the tablecloth. For the final touch, stick yellow 3x3-inch sticky notes onto the black cutouts to represent lit skyscraper windows. You get a six-foot-wide city backdrop in under 20 minutes.

### Sound Effect Action Bursts
Cut white and yellow poster board into jagged starburst shapes. Use a broad black marker to write classic comic action words like *BAM!*, *POW!*, or food-related phrases like *CRUNCH!* and *SLURP!*. Tape these directly to serving dishes, near board game stations, or on your front door as a welcome sign.

## Easy, Low-Cost Superhero Snack Hacks

Skip expensive customized bakery items. These three food hacks take standard grocery staples and turn them into thematic treats using minimal extra effort.

### "Kryptonite" Sweet & Salty Popcorn
- **Cost:** ~$5 total
- **Prep time:** 10 minutes
Pop two bags of plain or salted microwave popcorn and spread it out on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Melt half a cup of green candy melts in the microwave in 30-second bursts. Drizzle the bright green melted candy over the popcorn, sprinkle with coarse sugar if you have it, and let it cool for 10 minutes before breaking it into serving chunks.

### Captain America Shield Pizzas
- **Cost:** ~$8–$10 for two 12-inch pizzas
- **Prep time:** 15 minutes
Buy basic frozen cheese pizzas or pre-made pizza crusts with marinara sauce. Create concentric rings using sliced pepperoni and shredded mozzarella cheese: an outer pepperoni ring, an inner mozzarella ring, a smaller pepperoni ring, and a central star cut from a slice of provolone cheese using a paring knife. Bake according to package instructions.

### "Infinity Gem" Soda Punch
- **Cost:** ~$6
- **Prep time:** 5 minutes (plus freezing time)
Fill an ice cube tray with different colored liquids: red cranberry juice, blue Gatorade, green Hawaiian Punch, and orange mango juice. Freeze overnight. Right before guests arrive, dump the multi-colored ice cubes into a clear pitcher or punch bowl and pour 2-liter bottles of chilled ginger ale or lemon-lime soda over top. As the cubes melt, they subtly change the drink's flavor and color without watering it down.
